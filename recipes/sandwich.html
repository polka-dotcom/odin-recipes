French Dip Sandwich

Love this simple recipe that doesn't involve making a pot roast. I like sliced beef on mine, and am not looking for a pot roast sandwich. We have a store who makes real roast beefs and slices them in the deli, and you can have the doneness to your preference based on how they slice it.I like caramelized onions, swiss, and horseradish on mine. This is great as is, or with your favorite additions!

Find out how to make this amazing recipe!

Ingredients:

  • 4 hoagie rolls, split lengthwise
  • 1 (10.5 ounce) can beef consomme
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 pound thinly sliced deli roast beef
  • 8 slices provolone cheese

Steps:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. Open hoagie rolls and lay them out on a baking sheet.
  3. Heat beef consomm√© and water in a medium saucepan over medium-high heat to make a rich beef broth.
  4. Place roast beef in broth and warm for 3 minutes.
  5. Arrange meat on hoagie rolls and top each roll with 2 slices of provolone.
  6. Bake sandwiches in the preheated oven until cheese melts, about 5 minutes.
  7. Serve sandwiches with small bowls of warm broth for dipping.
